Electro Micro Surgery

Our flagship treatment — the cosmetic removal of benign growths including moles, skin tags, warts, verrucas, seborrhoeic keratosis, and cysts. Using advanced electro micro surgery techniques, Jilla delivers precise, controlled results with minimal scarring and rapid healing.

This non-surgical alternative to excision requires no stitches, leaves virtually no scarring, and most treatments are completed in a single session. Ideal for facial and body growths that cause cosmetic concern.

Precision Artistry

Medical Micropigmentation / MCA

Medical micropigmentation (MCA) is a specialised technique used for skin camouflage and the treatment of stretch marks. By implanting medical-grade pigments into the skin, we can effectively blend scars, burns, vitiligo patches, and stretch marks with your natural skin tone.

Jilla's artistic background and steady hand make her uniquely qualified for this precision work. Results are natural-looking and long-lasting, restoring confidence where skin irregularities once caused concern.

Skin Rejuvenation

Dermaroller

Dermaroller micro-needling is a clinically proven treatment that stimulates your skin's natural collagen and elastin production. Fine needles create controlled micro-injuries, triggering the body's healing response and resulting in firmer, smoother, more youthful skin.

Effective for reducing fine lines, wrinkles, acne scars, enlarged pores, and improving overall skin texture and radiance. Multiple sessions are recommended for optimal results, with each treatment building upon the last.

Body Contouring

Lipoglaze / Cryolipolysis

Lipoglaze uses cryolipolysis (fat freezing) technology to target and eliminate stubborn fat deposits that resist diet and exercise. The non-invasive procedure crystallises fat cells, which are then naturally eliminated by the body over the following weeks.

No needles, no anaesthesia, no downtime. Treatment areas include the abdomen, flanks, thighs, upper arms, and double chin. Results become visible within 2–4 weeks, with full effects typically seen after 8–12 weeks.
